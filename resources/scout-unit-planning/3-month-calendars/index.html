Three-Month Unit Planning Calendars

In Session 1 of Building Strong Units Through Program Calendars, we said that a calendar is a promise.

That’s great but how do we get started? This is the hardest part for most Scout leaders. Not because making a calendar is complicated but because they try to build a calendar for the whole year. Too many moving parts, too many unknowns, not enough time to get it right. The result: they procrastinate or they do it quickly just to have something or it stays in their head instead of becoming something that the whole team can follow.

Instead, build it in layers…

  • A short-term calendar—about 3 to 6 months that gives you stability. It’s what helps things run smoothly right now
  • A full-year calendar—about 12 months that gives families a sense of direction
  • And a longer-term view—12 to 18 months out that gives the unit continuity. It makes leadership transitions easier and keeps your program from starting over each year.

The good news: you don’t have to do all three right now. Start where you are.

For most units, that means building the next 90 days, not a year, not every detail. Three months is long enough to create consistency and short enough to actually finish. It’s a window where you can set your meeting rhythm, plan a few activities, and start giving families a clear picture of what to expect.

It doesn’t have to be perfect, just real. Because once something is in place, even a simple three-month plan, everything else becomes easier to build from there. And you start to fulfill the promise that the calendar makes.
